MAKING IMPOSSIBLE POSSIBLE.

Road safety and charity walk

The worrying trend of fatal road crashes is becoming a serious concern in the country. But of more concern is that half of these road deaths are as a result of mishandling and incorrect first aid given by the first responders, accounting for 57% of road crash deaths.
To cut down this trend of road fatalities, WELNESS SQUAD Rescue Team has initiated a Road Safety Project, aimed at training and giving emergency rescue kits to 2,000 first responders, including Bodaboda Operators, Matatu Drivers and Communities at black spots along the major highways. The training will help the responders to get knowledge on how to handle accident survivors and avoid causing more injuries, since most of the times they are the first ones to arrive at road crash scenes. The project is estimated to cost a total of KShs 12 Million in its first Phase of implementation.
As a charitable organization, we ordinarily rely on donors and well-wishers to fund our life-saving initiatives. It is in this respect that we have organized a Road Safety Walk to raise the funds for the project.
We welcome individuals and corporates to join us in this worthy cause. Every shilling counts and will go a long way in ensuring our roads are a lot safer.
As an equal opportunities employer, WELNESS SQUAD Rescue Team is committed to recruiting the best and brightest staff to help us continue to provide the highest level of service of humanity to all the people living in Kenya, schools, churches, our government, and corporates.
